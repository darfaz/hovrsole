The Science

Three systems. One breakthrough.

HOVRSOLE isn't just another cushioned shoe. It's a fundamentally different architecture ‚Äî an internal suspension system that decouples your foot from the ground.

Rigid Outer Frame

The exterior sole is a structural exoskeleton ‚Äî rigid along the sides and bottom perimeter. It absorbs terrain impact and channels force to the edges, away from the bottom of your foot.

Trampoline Inner Sole

Underneath your foot, the sole isn't solid ‚Äî it's a flexible, trampoline-like membrane suspended within the frame. It absorbs shock dynamically while keeping your foot elevated above the ground contact point.

Silicone Ankle Grip

Above the ankle, a high-grip silicone surface "grabs" and suspends the leg within the shoe. Your weight is held from above ‚Äî not pushed from below ‚Äî redirecting all pressure through the frame to the sole's edges.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

How does the suspension system actually work?+
Three integrated systems: (1) A rigid outer frame/exoskeleton that forms the shoe's structural shell. (2) A trampoline-like membrane under the foot that flexes dynamically instead of compressing. (3) A silicone ankle grip above the ankle that "catches" and suspends the leg. Together, they redirect your body weight through the frame to the sole's edges ‚Äî bypassing the bottom of your foot.
Is this a medical device? Do I need a prescription?+
No. HOVRSOLE is classified as performance/protective gear ‚Äî no FDA approval or prescription required. Think of it like a high-performance boot, not a medical brace. That said, many people with foot conditions (plantar fasciitis, diabetic neuropathy, post-surgical recovery) will benefit from the pressure offloading.
What's the patent status?+
We have a provisional patent filed covering the internal suspension architecture ‚Äî the combination of rigid external frame, trampoline sole membrane, and silicone ankle grip suspension system. The full utility patent application is in progress.
How is this different from HOKA, Nike Air, or other cushioned shoes?+
Fundamentally different approach. Cushioned shoes put soft material between your foot and the ground ‚Äî the force still hits the sole, just through a softer medium. HOVRSOLE structurally redirects force around and away from the sole entirely. It's the difference between a pillow and a suspension bridge.
